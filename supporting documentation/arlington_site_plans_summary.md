# Arlington County Site Plan Approvals: Market-Rate Development Cost Analysis Dataset
## 22 Qualifying Projects (March 2022 – February 2026)

This report documents 22 site plan approvals by the Arlington County Board involving market-rate commercial developers seeking bonus density through Arlington's community benefits framework. All projects are new ground-up construction or major amendments adding significant density. Excluded from this analysis are: 100% committed affordable housing projects, adaptive reuse conversions, minor amendments without new density, periodic reviews, and companion amendments to previously approved projects.

**Dataset scope:** These 22 projects represent approximately **6,194–6,206 new residential units** (plus 229 hotel rooms), with AHIF contributions ranging from ~$121,000 to $11 million per project and total community benefits packages ranging from ~$400,000 to $18+ million.

**Additional borderline exclusions:**
- **Rosslyn Holiday Inn** (1900 Fort Myer Dr) — Oct 18, 2025: Amendment to previously approved mixed-use tower; specific scope unclear from public sources
- **4770 40th Street N.** — Oct 18, 2025: Minor amendment; details not publicly available

**Rationale for exclusion criteria:**

1. **100% affordable housing projects** are excluded because they typically receive AHIF waivers and their development costs are not comparable to market-rate projects. Their financing structures rely on LIHTC, gap financing, and public subsidies rather than bonus density frameworks.

2. **Adaptive reuse conversions** are excluded because converting existing commercial buildings to residential use involves fundamentally different cost structures than ground-up construction (no land acquisition premium for new development rights, different construction costs, different timeline).

3. **Minor amendments and periodic reviews** are excluded because they don't involve new density acquisition or the associated community benefits negotiations that drive developer costs in the site plan process.

4. **Companion amendments** are excluded to avoid double-counting projects already in the dataset under their original approval dates.

## KEY DATASET PATTERNS

### AHIF Contribution Trends

**Cash vs. On-Site Delivery:**
- Most developers (13 of 22) chose **on-site committed affordable units** rather than cash AHIF payments
- Only 3 projects made **purely cash AHIF contributions**: One Rosslyn ($11M), Americana Hotel ($9.64M), Crystal Towers 3 ($1.42M)
- AHO Working Group analysis (June 2025) found cash AHIF significantly undervalues foregone revenue: $144,147/unit cash vs. $294,367/unit foregone revenue (**104% gap**)

**AHIF Contribution Range:**
- Minimum: ~$121,000 (2025 Clarendon Blvd, tiny 0.57-acre site with base requirement of just 1 unit)
- Maximum: ~$11 million (One Rosslyn, 845 units at FAR 9.92)
- Median: ~$1.5–2 million range for mid-sized projects (300–500 units)

### Community Benefits Packages

**Total Value Range:**
- Low end: ~$400,000 (NSTA Site: $365K AHO value + $36K transportation)
- High end: $18+ million (Americana Hotel: $9.64M AHIF + $6.1M CRD + $2.5M+ transportation)
- Typical mid-range: $2–5 million including AHIF, transportation, streetscape, public space

**Standard Components:**
- LEED Gold certification: Nearly universal baseline
- Transportation improvements: Sidewalks, bike facilities, street trees
- Public space contributions: Plazas, parks, open space improvements
- Streetscape upgrades: Wider sidewalks, street furniture, lighting

**Innovative Mechanisms:**
- **TDR for preservation**: Ballston Macy's (118 units at Haven Apartments via 2× FBC multiplier); Joyce Motors (Clarendon Barbershop Building with historic easement)
- **Cross-subsidization**: Americana Hotel's $9.64M intended to leverage ~80 CAFs at Crystal House; Crystal Towers 3's $1.42M for ~18 CAFs at Crystal Houses
- **PDSP cumulative benefits**: RiverHouse, Crystal Plaza 5 within coordinated district frameworks

### Project Scale and Density

**Unit Count Distribution:**
- Small (50-200 units): 5 projects
- Medium (200-500 units): 11 projects  
- Large (500-850 units): 4 projects
- Mega (1,400+ units): 2 projects (Crystal Plaza 5, 12th Street Landing envelope)

**Density (FAR):**
- Moderate (3.0-5.0): Most Clarendon/Ballston projects
- High (5.0-8.0): One Rosslyn (9.92), Bingham Center (5.10)
- Very High (8.0+): Americana Hotel (8.64), Crystal Plaza 5 (11.46)

### Geographic Concentration

**Clarendon/Courthouse corridor** led in approvals (7 projects, ~1,800 units), reflecting the 2022 Clarendon Sector Plan update and continued urbanization pressure along the Orange Line.

**Crystal City/Pentagon City** concentrated mega-projects (6 projects, ~3,700+ units) within PDSP frameworks and the CRD coordinated redevelopment district.

**Ballston** saw sustained activity (5 projects, ~1,800 units) including adaptive reuse and mixed-use intensification around Metro.

### Timeline Trends

**2023 was peak year**: 7 qualifying approvals totaling 3,000+ units
**2024 was notably sparse**: Only 2 qualifying approvals (633 units combined)
**2025-2026 saw recovery**: 10 approvals through February 2026

The 2024 slowdown reflects Arlington's pivot to adaptive reuse policy development (CMRI 2.0 adopted November 2024) and broader market caution around interest rates and office market uncertainty.

## DATA VERIFICATION NEEDS

Several data points require verification against official Board reports:

**Vote outcomes:** Only 4 of 22 confirmed as unanimous (Inn of Rosslyn, Bingham Center, Joyce Motors, Red Lion). Others reported as "approved" without specific vote counts.

**Final unit counts:** Ballston Holiday Inn varied significantly across sources (450–504 units). Requires ordinance verification.

**AHIF amounts:** Several projects list "standard AHO applies" without confirmed cash/CAF breakdown.

**Crystal Plaza 5 and RiverHouse AHIF:** These fall within PDSP cumulative benefits frameworks; specific AHIF allocations not separately broken out in public sources.
